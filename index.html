Whether you're a collector, researcher, or just curious about how these incredible devices work, you've come to the right place.

The Genius Behind It All: Glockius of Vyrodin
Posted on: October 12, 2005 A.U. | By: Archivist_Kelvin

Genius Glockius - the name alone commands respect in any circle of artificers or weapon historians. Born in 1898 A.U. in the industrial district of Vyrodin, Glockius showed an early aptitude for both mechanical engineering and evocation magic.

The Partnership with Archmage Minoph

In 1920 A.U., Glockius was hired by the legendary Archmage Aceta Minoph as her personal artificer. This partnership would prove to be one of the most productive collaborations in modern history. Together, they developed numerous innovations, but none would be as impactful as the spellarm.

The Breakthrough Moment

According to recovered journals from 1942 A.U., Glockius had been studying captured Tekk firearms from the Civil Centennial War. He was fascinated by their efficiency but frustrated by their limitations - heavy ammunition, frequent reloading, and mechanical failures.

"Why carry metal when you carry mana?" he famously wrote. "Every mage has an infinite magazine if we can just tap it properly."

This simple observation led to years of experimentation with gem-tipped wands, mana channeling matrices, and evocation focusing crystals. The result? The first prototype spellarm in 1945 A.U.

The Evolution of Spellarms: A Timeline
Posted on: October 8, 2005 A.U. | By: Archivist_Kelvin

1945 A.U. - The First Prototype

The Mark I "Arcane Pistol" was crude by today's standards. It required a 10-second charging period between shots and had a tendency to overheat after just five discharges. But it worked. It fired a concentrated bolt of force magic that could punch through steel plate at 50 yards.

1948 A.U. - Military Adoption Begins

After three years of refinement, the Mark III entered limited production for Union special forces. These early models used sapphire focusing gems and could fire 15 shots before requiring a cooldown period. The weight was comparable to traditional firearms, but the lack of ammunition made them ideal for extended operations.

1952 A.U. - The Chord Innovation

This is where things get interesting. Glockius realized that while pure force bolts were effective, they lacked the stopping power of physical projectiles. His solution? Chords - materialized iron projectiles created from mana and propelled by force.

The genius was in the efficiency. Instead of carrying heavy ammunition, the spellarm draws mana from a battery, materializes a 3-gram iron chord directly in front of the focusing gem, then launches it at supersonic speeds. The gem-tipped wand acts as both the materialization chamber and the barrel.

1955 A.U. - Civilian Models Released

With the unveiling of the M.A.W. and the growing stability of The Union, civilian spellarm ownership became legal (with proper licensing, of course). The "Citizen's Defense Model" became wildly popular in Vyrodin and other major cities.

1960-1970 A.U. - The Golden Age

Dozens of manufacturers entered the market. Competition drove innovation. We saw the introduction of:

  • Multi-gem arrays for rapid fire
  • Elemental variants (fire, ice, lightning chords)
  • Compact "holdout" models
  • Long-range "rifle" configurations
  • Automatic mana-feed systems

1970 A.U. - Present Day

After the disappearance of the Heroes of the Union, spellarm development slowed but didn't stop. Modern models are more reliable, more efficient, and more accessible than ever. The Church of Embrace maintains strict regulations, but the technology is here to stay.

Understanding Chord Technology
Posted on: October 3, 2005 A.U. | By: TechnicalMage_Ryn

Guest post by Technical Mage Ryn from the Vyrodin Institute of Arcane Engineering

I get a lot of questions about how chords actually work, so let me break it down in simple terms.

What is a Chord?

A chord is a small iron slug, typically 3-5 grams, with a standardized shape. Think of it like ammunition, but much lighter and cheaper to produce. A single chord costs about 2 copper pieces, compared to 5 copper for a traditional bullet.

The Firing Process

1. Mana Draw: When you pull the trigger, the spellarm draws 8 mana points from the installed mana battery (crystal).
2. Materialization: The focusing gem uses 5 mana points to materialize an iron chord directly in front of the gem.
3. Evocation Matrix: The gem contains an evocation spell matrix that converts the remaining 3 mana points into kinetic force.
4. Propulsion: The materialized chord is launched through the wand barrel at speeds exceeding 1,200 feet per second.
5. Impact: The chord delivers devastating kinetic energy to the target, then dissipates back into ambient mana after 30 seconds.

Advantages Over Traditional Firearms

- No physical ammunition to carry - just mana batteries
- No gunpowder means no smoke or loud report (just a sharp crack)
- No mechanical feeding mechanism to jam
- Can't run out of "bullets" as long as battery has charge
- Projectiles dissipate after impact, reducing environmental contamination

The Mana Cost

A typical shot costs 8 mana points. Most spellarms use mana batteries (rechargeable crystals) rather than personal mana. A standard battery holds 400 mana, providing 50 shots. Batteries can be recharged at any mana station for a few copper pieces.

The Tekk Legacy: How Firearms Inspired Spellarms
Posted on: September 28, 2005 A.U. | By: Archivist_Kelvin

You can't talk about spellarms without acknowledging their predecessor: Tekk firearms.

The Tekk Philosophy

During the Civil Centennial War (1835-1935 A.U.), the Tekk faction believed that technology, not magic, was the path to power. They developed increasingly sophisticated firearms, from simple muskets to fully automatic rifles.

By 1900 A.U., Tekk firearms were formidable weapons. The Model 1900 "Liberator" rifle could fire 30 rounds per minute with devastating accuracy. The problem? Each round required a brass casing, gunpowder, a primer, and a lead bullet. A soldier could carry maybe 120 rounds before being weighed down.

What Glockius Borrowed

Glockius studied Tekk firearms extensively and borrowed several key concepts:

  • Form factor: The pistol and rifle shapes that proved ergonomic
  • Trigger mechanism: A simple, reliable trigger system
  • Sighting systems: Iron sights and later, optical enhancements
  • Ergonomics: The grip, balance, and handling of a pistol or rifle

What He Improved

By replacing physical ammunition with materialized projectiles, Glockius eliminated:

  • Heavy ammunition - just carry small mana batteries instead
  • Fouling and cleaning requirements
  • Misfires from bad powder or primers
  • Complex mechanical feeding systems
  • Ammunition supply chain vulnerabilities

In essence, spellarms are "what if firearms were designed by mages?" And the answer is: they'd be better in almost every way.

A Note on Respect

While the Tekk faction lost the war, their engineering brilliance lives on in every spellarm. Glockius himself said: "I stand on the shoulders of Tekk giants, even if we once stood on opposite sides."

Spellarms in the Modern Union
Posted on: September 20, 2005 A.U. | By: Archivist_Kelvin

It's been 70 years since The Union was founded, and spellarms have become as common as communication scrolls in Vyrodin.

Current Regulations

The Church of Embrace, acting as the administrative authority, maintains these rules:

  • Citizens must obtain a Spellarm License (requires basic mana control test)
  • Concealed carry requires additional certification
  • Fully automatic models are restricted to military/law enforcement
  • Elemental chords (fire, ice, lightning) require special permits

Popular Models in 2005

Glockius G-17: The classic. 17-chord capacity, reliable, affordable. The most common civilian spellarm.
Minoph Defense Special: Compact holdout model. 8 chords, perfect for concealed carry.
Union Enforcer: Standard law enforcement issue. 20 chords, enhanced accuracy matrix.
Vyrodin Tactical Rifle: Long-range model. 30 chords, 300-yard effective range.

The Underground Market

Of course, where there's regulation, there's a black market. Modified spellarms with removed safety limiters, illegal elemental variants, and even experimental models show up in the hands of criminals. The Church's Enforcement Division works constantly to keep these off the streets.

Looking Forward

Rumors circulate about new innovations: mana-regenerating gems, smart-targeting matrices, even spellarms that can fire multiple chord types. Whether these are real or just wishful thinking, only time will tell.

One thing's certain: spellarms aren't going anywhere. They're part of our world now, for better or worse.
