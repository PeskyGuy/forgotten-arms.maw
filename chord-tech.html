Understanding Chord Technology
Technical Deep Dive - Last Updated: October 3, 2005 A.U.

A comprehensive guide to how chords work, written for enthusiasts and professionals alike.

What Exactly Is a Chord?

The term "chord" comes from the Old Tekk word "kord," meaning "core" or "heart." In spellarm terminology, a chord is a small, standardized projectile designed to be accelerated by evocation magic rather than chemical propellant.

Standard Chord Specifications:

  • Weight: 3-5 grams (compared to 8-12g for traditional bullets)
  • Material: Cold-forged iron with trace mithril (0.5%)
  • Length: 15mm
  • Diameter: 9mm
  • Shape: Aerodynamic teardrop with flat base

The Physics of Chord Acceleration

When you pull the trigger of a spellarm, a complex sequence occurs in milliseconds:

Step 1: Mana Draw
The trigger mechanism activates a mana siphon enchantment. This draws approximately 5 mana points from either:

  • The wielder's personal mana pool
  • An installed mana crystal
  • A combination of both (user preference)

Step 2: Matrix Activation
The drawn mana flows into the focusing gem at the wand's tip. This gem contains a pre-programmed evocation matrix - essentially a spell that's been crystallized into permanent form. The matrix converts raw mana into directional kinetic force.

Step 3: Chord Loading
Simultaneously, the magazine spring pushes a chord into the acceleration chamber. The chord seats against the base of the wand barrel, positioned directly behind the focusing gem.

Step 4: Acceleration
The evocation matrix releases its stored energy in a focused burst. This creates a "force tunnel" that accelerates the chord from 0 to 1,200+ feet per second in approximately 0.003 seconds. The wand barrel guides the chord, ensuring accuracy.

Step 5: Discharge
The chord exits the barrel with tremendous kinetic energy. The focusing gem briefly glows (blue for standard models, other colors for elemental variants) and requires 0.1 seconds to cool before the next shot.

Why Iron? The Metallurgy of Chords

Glockius experimented with dozens of materials before settling on iron. Here's why it works:

Density: Iron is dense enough to carry significant kinetic energy without being too heavy. Lead (used in traditional bullets) is denser but interferes with evocation magic.

Mana Conductivity: Pure iron has neutral mana conductivity - it neither absorbs nor repels magical energy. This makes it ideal for evocation acceleration.

Cost: Iron is cheap and abundant. A single gold piece can buy 500 standard chords.

The Mithril Trace: The 0.5% mithril content serves a specific purpose - it prevents chord deformation during acceleration. Pure iron chords would warp under the intense magical forces, affecting accuracy. Mithril reinforcement keeps them stable.

Chord Variants

While standard iron chords are most common, specialized variants exist:

Elemental Chords
These contain trace amounts of elemental crystals:

  • Fire Chords: Ruby dust core. Ignites on impact. (Restricted)
  • Ice Chords: Sapphire dust core. Freezes target area. (Restricted)
  • Lightning Chords: Topaz dust core. Delivers electrical shock. (Restricted)
Cost: 5-10 gold pieces per chord. Requires special permit.

Armor-Piercing Chords
Hardened steel core with tungsten tip. Designed to penetrate magical barriers and heavy armor. Military/Law enforcement only. Cost: 1 gold piece per chord.

Training Chords
Soft lead with reduced weight (1.5g). Lower velocity, less penetration. Used for practice and certification tests. Cost: 1 copper piece per chord.

Subsonic Chords
Heavier (7g) with flat nose. Travels below sound barrier for silent operation. Popular with hunters and certain professionals. Cost: 5 silver pieces per chord.

The Focusing Gem: Heart of the System

The focusing gem is what makes a spellarm work. Without it, you just have an expensive wand that shoots metal.

Gem Types and Performance:

Quartz (Budget Models):
- Mana efficiency: 60%
- Lifespan: ~5,000 shots
- Cost: 50 gold pieces
- Common in civilian models

Sapphire (Standard):
- Mana efficiency: 80%
- Lifespan: ~20,000 shots
- Cost: 200 gold pieces
- Military and professional standard

Diamond (Premium):
- Mana efficiency: 95%
- Lifespan: ~100,000 shots
- Cost: 1,000+ gold pieces
- Competition and elite military use

Enchanted Gems (Experimental):
- Mana efficiency: 99%+
- Lifespan: Potentially unlimited
- Cost: Priceless (not commercially available)
- Rumored to exist in prototype models

Mana Consumption and Efficiency

A common question: "How many shots can I fire?"

The answer depends on several factors:

Personal Mana Pool:
- Untrained civilian: 30-50 mana (6-10 shots)
- Basic training: 50-100 mana (10-20 shots)
- Professional: 100-200 mana (20-40 shots)
- Combat mage: 200-500 mana (40-100 shots)
- Master mage: 500+ mana (100+ shots)

Mana Crystals:
Most spellarms have a slot for a mana crystal, which acts as an external power source:

  • Minor crystal: 100 mana (20 shots) - 10 gold
  • Standard crystal: 250 mana (50 shots) - 25 gold
  • Greater crystal: 500 mana (100 shots) - 50 gold
  • Superior crystal: 1,000 mana (200 shots) - 100 gold

Regeneration:
Personal mana regenerates at approximately 10 points per hour of rest. Mana crystals don't regenerate - they're single-use.

Maintenance and Care

Spellarms require less maintenance than traditional firearms, but they're not maintenance-free:

After Every Use:

  • Wipe down the barrel with a clean cloth
  • Check the focusing gem for cracks or cloudiness
  • Ensure magazine spring tension is correct

Monthly:

  • Deep clean the acceleration chamber
  • Test mana draw efficiency (should be within 5% of baseline)
  • Inspect trigger mechanism for wear
  • Recharge or replace mana crystal

Annually:

  • Professional inspection by certified artificer
  • Focusing gem evaluation (replace if efficiency drops below 70%)
  • Complete disassembly and cleaning
  • Enchantment refresh (if applicable)

Common Problems and Solutions

Problem: Weak shots or reduced range
Cause: Degraded focusing gem or low mana
Solution: Replace gem or recharge mana source

Problem: Failure to feed chords
Cause: Weak magazine spring or debris in chamber
Solution: Replace spring or clean chamber

Problem: Overheating after few shots
Cause: Cracked focusing gem or mana leak
Solution: Immediate professional inspection - DO NOT FIRE

Problem: Inaccurate shots
Cause: Barrel misalignment or damaged chords
Solution: Professional barrel alignment or inspect chord quality

The Future of Chord Technology

Research continues into improved chord designs:

Self-Guiding Chords: Experimental chords with micro-enchantments that adjust trajectory mid-flight. Currently too expensive for practical use.

Mana-Reactive Chords: Chords that draw additional acceleration from ambient mana. Promising but unstable in low-mana environments.

Biodegradable Chords: Environmental concerns have led to research into chords that dissolve after impact. Early prototypes show promise.

Multi-Stage Chords: Chords that separate into multiple projectiles mid-flight. Banned in most jurisdictions but technically fascinating.
